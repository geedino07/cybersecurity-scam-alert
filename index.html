Tech Support Scam Awareness

Tech support scams trick people into believing their computer or account is infected or compromised. They often begin with alarming pop-ups, calls, or texts pretending to be from trusted companies like Microsoft or Apple. The goal is to create panic and urgency.

How the Scam Works

  • A fake alert claims your system is infected or at risk.
  • A “technician” convinces you to call a number or click a link.
  • You’re asked to install remote access software.
  • Fake problems appear, followed by a demand for payment.
  • Scammers steal money, personal information, or gain long-term access to your device.

What Scammers Want

  • Money: Fees paid via cards, gift cards, wire transfers, or crypto
  • Personal Information: Banking details, passwords, identity data
  • Device Access: To spy, steal files, or install real malware

How to Protect Yourself

  • Stay calm, legitimate companies don’t use pop-ups or unsolicited calls.
  • Never call numbers shown in pop-ups.
  • Don’t give remote access to unknown people.
  • If exposed, change passwords and monitor financial accounts immediately.

Why It’s Increasing

With Windows 11 updates and the end of Windows 10 support (October 2025), scammers exploit confusion around system upgrades and security warnings.

Bottom Line

Tech support scams rely on fear and trust. Legitimate companies will never demand payment or remote access unexpectedly. Skepticism and caution are your best defenses.
